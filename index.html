What is E-Waste?

E-waste refers to discarded electrical or electronic devices — from smartphones and laptops to TVs and batteries. It’s the world’s fastest-growing waste stream, containing toxic substances (lead, mercury, cadmium) that harm human health and the environment.

Key Causes

  • Rapid product cycles: new models released annually.
  • High consumer demand for the latest gadgets.
  • Poor repairability and non-modular designs.
  • Lack of convenient recycling options.

Environmental & Health Impacts

Improper disposal (landfills, informal burning) releases toxic metals and plastics into soil, air, and water. Workers in informal recycling face respirational, neurological, and skin diseases.

Soil & Water

Heavy metals leach into groundwater, bioaccumulate in food chains.

Air Pollution

Burning plastics emits dioxins and furans, causing respiratory issues.

Human Health

Exposure leads to developmental, neurological, and reproductive disorders.
